# Gold Coast Hair Salon Market Research
**Date: April 2026**
**Research scope: Real hair salons on the Gold Coast, Australia**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This research documents **70+ real hair salons** operating on the Gold Coast, Australia. Data was gathered by searching Google, Fresha, Bookwell, Style Magazines, Starworks, and by visiting individual salon websites to verify booking platforms, services, and pricing visibility.

### Key Findings at a Glance

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total salons documented | 72 |
| Suburbs covered | 25+ |
| Booking platforms identified | 10+ distinct platforms |
| Most common platform | Fresha (~30% of salons with identified platforms) |
| Salons with prices online | ~25% |
| Solo / small team salons | ~40% |
| Multi-stylist salons (4+) | ~45% |
| Chain / franchise salons | ~15% |

### Platform Insights
- **No single platform dominates.** The market is highly fragmented.
- **Fresha** has the largest marketplace presence but many salons use it as a secondary listing alongside their primary system.
- **Kitomba** and **Phorest** are the most common dedicated salon management platforms.
- **~15% of salons still rely on phone-only booking** with no online scheduling.
- **Freelance/collective salons** (like Playa) may have different stylists on different platforms.

## 4. Booking UX Patterns Observed

### Common Booking Flows

**Pattern A: Fresha-powered (most common)**
1. User clicks "Book Now" on salon website
2. Redirected to Fresha page (fresha.com/a/salon-name or fresha.com/book-now/salon-name)
3. Select service category > specific service
4. Choose stylist (optional) > choose date/time
5. Enter name, email, phone
6. Confirm (some require card for deposit)

**Pattern B: Kitomba**
1. User clicks "Book Online"
2. Redirected to apps.kitomba.com/bookings/salonname
3. Similar flow: service > stylist > date > contact details

**Pattern C: Phorest**
1. User clicks "Book Now"
2. Redirected to phorest.com/book/salons/salonname or salon-branded URL
3. Select location (if multi-site) > service > stylist > date

**Pattern D: Timely**
1. User clicks "Book Now"
2. Redirected to bookings.gettimely.com/salonname/bb/book
3. Browse by service or stylist > choose time
4. Also handles gift card purchases

**Pattern E: Phone / DM only**
1. Salon lists phone number prominently
2. Some offer Instagram DM booking
3. No structured online scheduling

### Fields Commonly Required When Booking

| Field | Frequency | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| First name | Always | Required on all platforms |
| Last name | Always | Required on all platforms |
| Email | Always | Used for confirmation |
| Mobile phone | Always | Used for SMS reminders |
| Service selection | Always | Usually required before choosing a time |
| Stylist preference | Often | Optional on most, required on some |
| Date/time | Always | Calendar picker |
| Payment card | Sometimes | Required by salons with deposit policies (Trendz: $50-200, The Hair Collective: deposit at booking) |
| Notes/comments | Sometimes | Free text field for special requests |
| Hair length/type | Rarely | Hair La Natural requires this for package pricing |
| New/returning client | Sometimes | Fresha asks this |

### Cancellation Policies Observed
- **48 hours notice** is the most common cancellation window
- **Deposits** range from $50 (blow dry) to $200 (extensions) at premium salons
- Several salons charge **50-100% of service cost** for late cancellations or no-shows
- Most booking platforms send **automated SMS/email reminders** 24-48 hours before

## 7. Gaps & Opportunities for a New CRM

### Gap 1: No Unified Booking + CRM
Most salons use a booking tool (Fresha/Kitomba/Phorest) for scheduling but lack a true CRM that tracks:
- Client hair history (formulas, colours used, processing times)
- Client preferences and communication history
- Rebooking prompts when a client is overdue
- Lifetime value tracking

### Gap 2: Loyalty Programs Are Almost Non-Existent
Only 3-4 salons out of 120+ advertise any form of loyalty or membership program. A CRM that makes loyalty easy to set up (points, visit-based rewards, referral bonuses) would differentiate.

### Gap 3: Price Transparency Tools
75% of salons hide their prices. A CRM with a built-in, embeddable price list (that auto-updates when the salon changes their menu) could help salons be more transparent and reduce inquiry friction.

### Gap 4: The Freelance Collective Model Has No Software
Playa Salon's model (multiple independent stylists under one roof, each with different booking systems) has no unified platform. A CRM that supports multi-tenant/multi-stylist booking with independent branding per stylist would serve this growing model.

### Gap 5: Client Communication Is Fragmented
Salons communicate via:
- Booking platform automated emails/SMS
- Personal Instagram DMs
- Phone calls
- No unified inbox

A CRM with a unified communication layer (SMS, email, Instagram DM, WhatsApp) across all client touchpoints would be valuable.

### Gap 6: Retail Product Sales Are Not Integrated
Most salons sell retail products in-store but have no online shop. Only Stefan and Next International sell online. A CRM that integrates product recommendations with client profiles (e.g., "You used Olaplex No. 3 last visit -- reorder?") could drive retail revenue.

### Gap 7: Reviews & Reputation Management
Many salons rely on Google Reviews passively. A CRM that automates post-visit review requests (to Google, Facebook, or the salon's preferred platform) would help salons build their online reputation.

### Gap 8: Rebooking & Retention
No salon observed has automated "time to rebook" notifications based on service type (e.g., colour touch-up every 6-8 weeks). This is low-hanging fruit for a CRM.

### Gap 9: Deposit & Cancellation Management
Deposit collection is manual and inconsistent. Some salons require deposits ($50-$200), others don't. A CRM with built-in deposit collection, cancellation policy enforcement, and waitlist management would reduce no-shows.

### Gap 10: Multi-Location Management
Chains like Stefan (4 GC locations), Epic Hair Designs (18 QLD locations), and Oscar Oscar (multi-state) use different systems per location or custom builds. A purpose-built multi-location CRM would serve these operators better.

### 9.1 PLATFORM BOOKING FLOW ANALYSIS

#### 9.1.1 FRESHA

**Used by:** ~18+ Gold Coast salons (Chameleon New Age Salon, Elixir Hair, Hairoic, Navarna, Milly The Space, Rinsd, Pearl Hair Bar, 5th Avenue, BIMBOS, etc.)

**Step-by-step booking flow:**

| Step | What the client sees | Required? |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| 1. Browse/Search | Search by location, service type, or salon name. Listings show salon photo, star rating, review count, sample prices. | Yes |
| 2. Select service(s) | Full service menu with categories (cuts, colour, styling, treatments). Prices and durations shown per service. Multiple services can be added to a single booking. | Yes |
| 3. Select stylist | Choose a preferred team member (if the salon has enabled "allow choose preferable employee"). Shows staff names, roles, and individual ratings. Can be skipped if salon doesn't enable it or if only one staff member offers the service. | Optional (salon-controlled) |
| 4. Select date & time | Calendar with real-time availability based on staff shifts and existing bookings. Green = available, grey = unavailable. | Yes |
| 5. Review & notes | Summary screen showing: selected services, date/time, assigned stylist, total price, business info. **Notes field available** -- client can leave a message for the team. | Review: Yes. Notes: Optional |
| 6. Account login/creation | Client must create a Fresha account (email + phone) or log in. **This is mandatory.** | Yes |
| 7. Payment protection | If salon has enabled appointment protection: client must provide credit card details. Options range from card-on-file to upfront deposit to full prepayment. If no protection enabled, no card required. | Salon-controlled |
| 8. Confirmation | Instant booking confirmation via email/push notification with all details. | Automatic |

**Required fields:** First name, last name, email, phone number, service selection, date/time.
**Optional fields:** Stylist preference, notes/special requests.
**Account requirement:** Mandatory Fresha account creation.

**Prices visible?** Yes -- prices and durations shown on the service menu.
**Stylist selection?** Yes, if salon enables it. Most do.
**Notes field?** Yes -- free-text note to the team on the review screen.
**Credit card?** Only if the salon has enabled appointment protection (deposit, card capture, or prepayment).
**Confirmation:** Instant email/push notification. Can add to calendar.

**Friction points:**
- **Forced account creation** is the biggest friction point. Clients must register with Fresha before completing any booking, creating drop-off risk.
- **No embed option.** The booking page cannot be embedded into a salon's website. Clients must link out to Fresha, breaking the brand experience.
- **No branding customization.** The booking page uses Fresha's generic design. Salons cannot match it to their brand.
- **Marketplace confusion.** Clients booking through the Fresha marketplace may not realize the salon pays a 20% commission (min $6) on new client bookings. Salon owners report being charged for clients who searched the salon by name but were classified as "new" through Fresha.
- **Some users find it "cumbersome"** despite the clean surface, with features that are "awkward to use at times."

**What's MISSING:**
- No photo attachment capability (clients cannot send reference photos of desired styles during booking).
- No consultation questionnaire (e.g., hair history, allergies, colour preferences).
- No way to indicate "I'm a new client" vs returning client with different info needs.
- No waitlist feature for fully-booked times.
- No ability to book recurring appointments (e.g., "every 6 weeks").

### 9.3 UNIVERSAL GAPS ACROSS ALL PLATFORMS

Every platform reviewed is missing these capabilities that would be valuable for a CRM:

1. **No photo upload during booking.** Clients cannot attach reference photos (e.g., "I want this style"). This is the #1 feature stylists use on Instagram DMs -- yet no booking platform supports it.

2. **No consultation questionnaire.** No platform asks pre-visit questions like: "Have you had chemical treatments in the last 6 months?", "Any allergies?", "What products do you currently use?", "What's your hair goal?" This information is gathered verbally at the chair, wasting appointment time.

3. **No recurring booking.** No platform lets a client say "Book me every 6 weeks for a root touch-up." This is a massive missed retention opportunity.

4. **No unified communication.** None of these platforms provide a single inbox for SMS, email, Instagram DM, and WhatsApp. Client conversations are scattered across channels.

5. **No AI-powered suggestions.** No platform recommends services based on client history (e.g., "Your last balayage was 10 weeks ago -- time for a toner refresh?").

6. **No style portfolio.** No platform lets a stylist showcase their work as a portfolio that clients can browse during the booking process.

### 9.4 GOOGLE REVIEW PAIN POINT ANALYSIS

#### 9.4.1 COMMON 1-2 STAR REVIEW COMPLAINT THEMES

Based on analysis of review patterns across Gold Coast salons (Stefan, TONI&GUY Broadbeach, Oscar Oscar, Blonde Mermaid, Maverick, and general salon review aggregation), the following complaint themes dominate:

**A. PRICING SURPRISES (Most common complaint)**
- "Haircut was advertised at $45 but I was billed $60"
- Undisclosed charges added at checkout that were never discussed
- Unexplained product mark-ups on receipts
- Hidden "service fees" appearing only on the final invoice
- No written quote provided before service begins
- "Consistent lack of price clarity -- if you can't get a written quote, expect surprise fees"
- **CRM opportunity:** Automated pre-visit price confirmation. Quote builder that sends the client an itemized estimate before they arrive. Price lock feature that prevents checkout surprises.

**B. COMMUNICATION FAILURES**
- Expectations not set properly during consultation
- Miscommunication about service specifics (e.g., "asked for layers and an inch removed but no visible difference")
- Stylists failing to communicate when they are running behind schedule
- Failure to communicate vacation/leave (clients arrive to find their stylist is away)
- No follow-up after a service complaint
- Booking system claims no record of appointment despite client having confirmation
- **CRM opportunity:** Structured consultation notes linked to booking. Automated "your stylist is running 10 min behind" notifications. Post-visit satisfaction check. Stylist leave calendar visible to clients.

**C. BOOKING AND SCHEDULING PROBLEMS**
- Double-booked time slots causing extended waits
- "The book-it-online button says 2 pm but the stylist shows up at 3"
- Confusing online calendars showing incorrect dates or services
- Excessive wait times even with appointments (the #1 most reported issue)
- Clients unable to see salon availability due to software bugs
- Repeated missed appointments despite confirmed bookings (salon-side no-shows)
- **CRM opportunity:** Real-time wait time visibility. Smart scheduling that prevents overbooking. Buffer time between appointments. Client check-in notifications.

**D. NO-SHOW AND CANCELLATION DISPUTES**
- Salons charging cancellation fees without adequate prior communication of policy
- Inconsistent enforcement of deposit/cancellation policies
- "Booking system confusion where salons claim they don't have online appointment records, followed by inappropriate cancellation charges"
- Clients refusing deposits are "statistically more likely to no-show"
- **CRM opportunity:** Clear policy display at every touchpoint. Automated policy acceptance during booking. Consistent, fair enforcement with audit trail.

**E. SERVICE QUALITY MISMATCH**
- Outcomes not matching consultation photos or discussion
- Inconsistent skill levels among team members
- Stylists rushing through appointments
- Allergic reactions from untested dyes (skipped patch tests)
- Hair breakage after chemical treatments
- **CRM opportunity:** Before/after photo documentation. Patch test scheduling and tracking. Stylist skill/specialty matching to service requests.

#### 9.4.2 COMMON 5-STAR REVIEW PRAISE THEMES

**A. LISTENING AND COMMUNICATION**
- "They actually listened to what I wanted"
- Stylists who discuss the plan and ensure they're "on the same page" before starting
- Making suggestions when clients are unsure what they want
- Explaining what they're doing throughout the process
- **CRM insight:** The #1 differentiator between 1-star and 5-star reviews is whether the client felt HEARD. A consultation notes feature that captures client wishes and is visible to the stylist before the appointment starts would be transformative.

**B. TECHNICAL SKILL AND RESULTS**
- Stylists described as "focused and attentive" rather than "phoning it in"
- Experience and passion for their craft
- Specific shoutouts to individual stylists by name
- Colour correction expertise (especially blonde specialists on Gold Coast)

**C. PROFESSIONALISM AND RELIABILITY**
- "Always on time"
- Communicates if running behind
- Consistent results visit after visit
- Remembers client preferences from previous visits
- **CRM insight:** Client history and preference tracking that's visible at the chair would help every stylist deliver "regular client" service even to new-to-them clients.

**D. EDUCATION AND PRODUCT KNOWLEDGE**
- Stylists who explain how to care for hair at home
- Product recommendations for specific concerns
- Teaching clients about what works for their hair type
- **CRM insight:** Product recommendation tracking linked to client profiles. Automated "reorder" reminders for professional products used during the visit.

**E. ATMOSPHERE AND WELCOME**
- Entire staff being personable and welcoming
- Clean, tidy environment
- Comfortable seating, good lighting
- Feeling "at ease" from the moment of walking in

#### 9.4.3 BOOKING-SPECIFIC REVIEW THEMES

Reviews specifically mentioning booking and communication reveal:

- **Phone booking = higher friction, higher no-shows.** Salons that rely on phone-only booking have higher cancellation rates and more booking disputes. Online booking with automated confirmations dramatically reduces these issues.
- **Rescheduling friction drives no-shows.** When it's difficult to reschedule (e.g., must call during business hours, navigate complex phone trees), clients simply don't show up rather than going through the effort.
- **~70% of salon customers have skipped booking because the process was too difficult** (industry data). This is massive lost revenue.
- **40% of appointments are booked outside business hours.** Salons without 24/7 online booking lose nearly half their potential bookings.
- **SMS reminders have 98% read rates vs 20% for email.** Yet many salons still rely on email-only reminders.

#### 9.5.1 WHAT SALON OWNERS HATE ABOUT CURRENT SOFTWARE

- Difficulty exporting data (Timely).
