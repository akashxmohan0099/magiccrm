# Gold Coast Beauty & Wellness Market Research
## Real Business Data for CRM Persona Validation
### Research Date: April 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Total businesses identified: 280+** across 15+ distinct categories on the Gold Coast, Australia.

**Key finding: The current 6 persona categories (Hair & Cuts, Nails, Lashes & Brows, Skin & Body, Makeup, Multi-Service) MISS several major market segments.** At least 4-5 additional categories have significant enough business density to warrant dedicated personas.

| Nails | Good coverage | Adequate |

| Multi-Service | Good concept | Works as catch-all |

### MISSING MAJOR CATEGORIES:

#### 1. BARBERS / BARBERSHOPS (85+ businesses -- CRITICAL MISS)
This is the single biggest gap. With 85+ businesses on the Gold Coast alone, barbers represent one of the largest segments in the beauty/wellness market. They have completely different:
- Service menus (cuts, fades, beard trims, hot towel shaves)
- Booking patterns (walk-in heavy)
- Client demographics (predominantly male)
- Business culture and branding
- Software needs (queue management vs appointment booking)

#### 2. COSMETIC TATTOO / PERMANENT MAKEUP (15+ businesses -- SIGNIFICANT MISS)
Distinct from both traditional tattoo and lash/brow businesses:
- Different service model (single long appointments, 2-3 sessions)
- Different pricing (higher ticket, $300-$800 per session)
- Different booking needs (consultations, before/after photos, consent forms)
- Requires specific features (healing timeline tracking, touch-up reminders)

#### 3. SKIN CLINICS / AESTHETICS / INJECTABLES (35+ businesses -- SIGNIFICANT MISS)
Currently lumped with "Skin & Body" but these are medical-adjacent businesses:
- Often nurse-led or doctor-led
- Require consent forms, medical history
- Different pricing model (consultations, treatment plans)
- Compliance/regulation requirements
- Very different from a day spa or beauty salon

#### 4. TATTOO STUDIOS (15+ businesses -- MODERATE MISS)
While adjacent to beauty/wellness, tattoo studios:
- Have unique booking needs (consultations, deposits, design approvals)
- Different pricing model (hourly rates, custom quotes)
- Portfolio/gallery-focused marketing
- Multi-artist studio management

#### 5. DAY SPAS / MASSAGE (30+ businesses -- MODERATE MISS)
Currently lumped with "Skin & Body" but very different:
- Package-based booking (spa packages, couples experiences)
- Gift voucher heavy
- Often hotel/resort affiliated
- Different scheduling (longer appointments, room management)

#### 6. TANNING / SPRAY TAN (10+ businesses)
Small but distinct segment with unique needs:
- Very short appointment times (10-15 min)
- Mobile/on-location service model common
- Event-driven (weddings, formals)
- Often combined with MUA services

#### 7. WELLNESS / HOLISTIC / RECOVERY (15+ businesses)
Growing segment that includes:
- Naturopaths, acupuncturists, herbalists
- Float therapy, cryotherapy, contrast therapy
- Different client relationship (health-focused, ongoing treatment plans)
- Often require practitioner credentials/certifications

## KEY MARKET INSIGHTS

1. **The Gold Coast beauty market is MASSIVE** -- Fresha alone lists 170+ beauty salons, and that is just one booking platform. The total market likely exceeds 500+ distinct beauty/wellness businesses.

2. **Solo operators dominate** -- Especially in nails, lashes, cosmetic tattoo, makeup, and spray tanning. Many work from home studios, are mobile, or rent chairs/rooms.

3. **Barbers are the elephant in the room** -- 85+ businesses that your current persona list completely ignores. This is arguably the #2 or #3 largest segment after hair salons and nail salons.

4. **"Skin & Body" is too broad** -- The actual market splits into at least 3 distinct segments: clinical skin/aesthetics (nurse-led), day spas/massage (relaxation-focused), and holistic wellness. These businesses have fundamentally different operational models.

5. **Cosmetic tattoo is its own category** -- It overlaps with lash/brow specialists but has unique business needs (longer sessions, fewer daily clients, healing management, before/after tracking).

6. **Mobile and home-based businesses are HUGE** -- Across nails, lashes, makeup, spray tan, and massage, a significant portion of the market operates without a traditional shopfront. CRM features for mobile operators (location tracking, travel radius, house-call booking) would serve a large segment.

7. **Multi-service overlap is the norm** -- Very few businesses fit neatly into one category. The persona system needs to handle combinations gracefully (e.g., a beauty salon that does nails + lashes + brows + waxing + facials).

8. **Australian beauty market = $12.4B** nationally (2025), with the Gold Coast being one of the most dense beauty markets in Australia given its tourism-driven, image-conscious demographic.
